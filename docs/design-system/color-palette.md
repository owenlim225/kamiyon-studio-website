# Purpose

This document defines the official color system for Kamiyon Studio.

It establishes the primary, secondary, and supporting colors used across branding, products, websites, presentations, publications, marketing materials, merchandise, and user interfaces.

A consistent color system strengthens brand recognition and creates a cohesive visual experience.

---

# Brand Color Philosophy

Kamiyon's palette is designed to communicate two complementary qualities:

* **Playful creativity**, inspired by games, curiosity, and exploration.
* **Professional craftsmanship**, expressed through clean compositions and premium visual presentation.

The palette should feel warm, approachable, modern, and timeless.

---

# Primary Colors

These colors form the foundation of the Kamiyon brand.

## Sakura Pink

**Role**

Primary brand color.

Represents:

* Creativity
* Curiosity
* Growth
* Optimism
* Playfulness

Use for:

* Primary buttons
* Brand highlights
* Icons
* Calls-to-action
* Key illustrations
* Mascot accents

---

## Warm Ivory

**Role**

Primary light background.

Represents:

* Simplicity
* Warmth
* Accessibility
* Clarity

Use for:

* Website backgrounds
* Documentation
* Presentation slides
* Product interfaces
* Printed materials

---

## Charcoal

**Role**

Primary dark neutral.

Represents:

* Professionalism
* Stability
* Readability

Use for:

* Body text
* Navigation
* Icons
* Interface elements
* Secondary backgrounds

---

## Deep Indigo

**Role**

Primary accent dark.

Represents:

* Innovation
* Depth
* Trust
* Technology

Use for:

* Headers
* Hero sections
* Premium layouts
* Dark mode applications
* Presentation backgrounds

---

# Accent Color

## Soft Gold

**Role**

Premium accent.

Represents:

* Excellence
* Achievement
* Craftsmanship
* Celebration

Use sparingly for:

* Awards
* Highlights
* Decorative elements
* Premium UI details
* Achievement indicators

Gold should enhance—not dominate—the visual hierarchy.

---

# Supporting Colors

Additional colors may be introduced within products and marketing materials when they support the overall composition.

Supporting colors should:

* Harmonize with the primary palette.
* Never compete with Sakura Pink as the primary brand color.
* Maintain accessibility and readability.
* Preserve a clean visual identity.

---

# Color Hierarchy

The preferred order of visual emphasis is:

1. Sakura Pink
2. Deep Indigo
3. Charcoal
4. Warm Ivory
5. Soft Gold

This hierarchy should remain consistent across brand applications.

---

# User Interface Guidelines

For digital products:

* Warm Ivory should serve as the primary light background.
* Charcoal should be the default text color.
* Sakura Pink should emphasize important interactive elements.
* Deep Indigo should define structure and visual depth.
* Soft Gold should highlight exceptional moments such as achievements or milestones.

The interface should feel inviting without becoming visually overwhelming.

---

# Accessibility

Every color combination should maintain sufficient contrast for readability.

Design decisions should prioritize:

* Legible typography.
* Clear visual hierarchy.
* Color accessibility.
* Inclusive user experiences.

Color should reinforce meaning but should never be the only indicator of information.

---

# Emotional Associations

The palette should evoke feelings of:

* Curiosity.
* Creativity.
* Comfort.
* Trust.
* Exploration.
* Optimism.
* Quality.
* Craftsmanship.

These emotional qualities support Kamiyon's mission and brand personality.

---

# Consistency Across Products

While individual products may develop their own visual identities, they should still retain subtle connections to the Kamiyon brand.

This may be achieved through:

* Shared accent colors.
* Consistent UI components.
* Brand splash screens.
* Marketing materials.
* Publisher branding.

The parent brand should remain recognizable without restricting creative freedom.

---

# Merchandise

On merchandise, the palette should preserve a premium appearance.

Preferred applications include:

* Apparel.
* Stickers.
* Packaging.
* Event materials.
* Office supplies.
* Collectibles.
* Mascot merchandise.

High-quality color reproduction should be prioritized to maintain brand consistency.

---

# Future Evolution

The core palette is intended to remain stable over time.

Additional supporting colors may be introduced as the brand expands into new products or campaigns, provided they complement the established visual identity rather than replace it.

Brand recognition grows through consistency.

---

# Relationship to the Brand

The Kamiyon color palette reflects the studio's identity.

It balances creativity with professionalism, warmth with precision, and playfulness with craftsmanship.

These colors should consistently reinforce the experience people associate with Kamiyon Studio across every touchpoint.

---

# Final Principle

Color is one of the fastest ways people recognize a brand.

By applying the Kamiyon palette thoughtfully and consistently, every product, presentation, website, and piece of merchandise becomes part of a unified visual identity that communicates creativity, quality, and a commitment to meaningful interactive experiences.
