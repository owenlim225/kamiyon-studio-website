# Company Culture

> **Culture is not what we write on a wall—it is what we consistently practice when nobody is watching.**

---

# Purpose

This document defines the culture of Kamiyon Studio.

It establishes the behaviors, expectations, and principles that shape how we work together, make decisions, solve problems, and represent the company.

Products may evolve.

Technology will change.

Markets will shift.

Our culture should remain our strongest competitive advantage.

---

# Our Philosophy

Kamiyon Studio believes that exceptional products are created by exceptional teams.

Talent alone is not enough.

Great teams are built upon:

* Trust
* Curiosity
* Respect
* Ownership
* Communication
* Continuous learning

Every member contributes to the culture.

Culture is everyone's responsibility.

---

# What Defines Kamiyon

At its core, Kamiyon is a company of builders.

We are designers, artists, programmers, marketers, educators, and problem-solvers united by a shared mission to create meaningful interactive experiences.

We value people who are eager to learn more than people who claim to know everything.

We celebrate curiosity over ego.

---

# Our Culture Pillars

## 1. We Stay Curious

Learning never stops.

Every project is an opportunity to discover something new.

We encourage teammates to explore:

* New technologies
* New art styles
* Different game genres
* Educational methods
* Business ideas
* Research
* Community feedback

Nobody is expected to know everything.

Everyone is expected to keep learning.

---

## 2. We Build With Purpose

We don't build products simply because they are technically impressive.

Every feature should have a reason.

Every mechanic should support an experience.

Every technology should solve a problem.

Purpose always comes before implementation.

---

## 3. We Finish What We Start

Ideas are abundant.

Execution creates value.

A finished prototype teaches more than an unfinished masterpiece.

We value consistency over bursts of motivation.

Shipping matters.

---

## 4. We Learn Together

Knowledge should never become a competitive advantage within the team.

If one person learns something valuable, everyone benefits.

We encourage:

* Internal documentation
* Knowledge sharing
* Code reviews
* Design critiques
* Technical discussions
* Mentorship

The company grows when its people grow.

---

## 5. We Communicate Openly

Good communication prevents great problems.

We believe in:

* Honest feedback
* Respectful disagreement
* Clear expectations
* Early communication
* Active listening

Silence creates confusion.

Transparency builds trust.

---

## 6. We Own Our Work

Every project carries the Kamiyon name.

Ownership means:

* Taking responsibility.
* Fixing mistakes.
* Asking for help early.
* Delivering our best work.
* Caring about the final experience.

We own both our successes and our failures.

---

# How We Work Together

## Collaboration Before Competition

We compete with our previous selves—not each other.

Healthy discussion is encouraged.

Personal rivalry is not.

The best idea wins regardless of who suggested it.

---

## Respect Every Discipline

Programming.

Art.

Design.

Marketing.

Community.

Business.

Every discipline contributes to the final product.

No role exists in isolation.

We succeed together.

---

## Constructive Feedback

Feedback exists to improve the work—not diminish the person.

When giving feedback:

* Be specific.
* Be respectful.
* Explain why.
* Offer alternatives.
* Focus on the outcome.

When receiving feedback:

* Listen first.
* Avoid becoming defensive.
* Ask questions.
* Learn from it.
* Thank the person giving it.

Great products require many revisions.

---

# Decision Making

Whenever possible, decisions should be guided by evidence rather than assumptions.

We ask:

* What problem are we solving?
* Who benefits?
* What are the trade-offs?
* What have we learned?
* Does this align with our mission?

If data is unavailable, we build prototypes and learn quickly.

---

# How We Handle Mistakes

Mistakes are inevitable.

Repeating preventable mistakes is optional.

When something goes wrong:

1. Understand what happened.
2. Fix the issue.
3. Document the lesson.
4. Improve the process.
5. Share the knowledge.

Blame solves nothing.

Learning improves everything.

---

# Innovation Culture

Innovation is encouraged throughout the company.

Everyone is welcome to propose:

* Product ideas
* New technologies
* Better workflows
* Marketing experiments
* Design improvements
* Community initiatives

Ideas are evaluated by merit—not by job title.

---

# Documentation Culture

If knowledge exists only in one person's mind, the company is vulnerable.

Documentation is considered part of the work—not an optional task.

We strive to document:

* Decisions
* Systems
* Designs
* Processes
* Lessons learned
* Technical knowledge

Good documentation enables faster growth and stronger collaboration.

---

# Learning Culture

Every team member is expected to invest in continuous improvement.

Learning may include:

* Reading
* Research
* Courses
* Conferences
* Hackathons
* Game jams
* Community events
* Personal projects

Growth is part of the job.

---

# Community Culture

Kamiyon believes that success should benefit more than the company itself.

Whenever possible, we contribute back through:

* Mentorship
* Educational initiatives
* Workshops
* Community events
* Open knowledge sharing
* Student engagement

The communities that helped us grow deserve our continued support.

---

# Leadership Philosophy

Leadership at Kamiyon is based on service.

Leaders exist to:

* Remove obstacles.
* Support teammates.
* Create clarity.
* Encourage growth.
* Make difficult decisions.
* Protect the company's culture.

Leadership is demonstrated through action—not authority.

---

# Excellence

We pursue excellence—not perfection.

Perfection often delays progress.

Excellence means delivering work we are genuinely proud to attach the Kamiyon name to while continuously improving through iteration.

Finished and refined is better than endlessly unfinished.

---

# What We Celebrate

At Kamiyon, we celebrate:

* Learning something new.
* Shipping a project.
* Helping a teammate.
* Solving difficult problems.
* Mentoring others.
* Creative experimentation.
* Honest feedback.
* Personal growth.
* Community impact.

Recognition should reinforce the culture we want to build.

---

# What We Don't Tolerate

The following behaviors are inconsistent with Kamiyon's culture:

* Dishonesty.
* Disrespect.
* Toxic competition.
* Knowledge hoarding.
* Ego-driven decision making.
* Discrimination or harassment.
* Blaming others instead of solving problems.
* Sacrificing long-term quality for short-term convenience.

Culture is protected through consistent action, not occasional reminders.

---

# Our Promise

As Kamiyon grows, new teammates, partners, and technologies will become part of our journey.

Our culture should remain recognizable regardless of size.

We promise to remain:

* Curious enough to keep learning.
* Humble enough to keep improving.
* Brave enough to keep experimenting.
* Responsible enough to own our work.
* Ambitious enough to compete globally.
* Proud enough to carry our Filipino identity into everything we create.

If we preserve these values, we believe Kamiyon Studio will become more than a successful company.

It will become a place where people create meaningful work, grow together, and leave a lasting impact on the world.
