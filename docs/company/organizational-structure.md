# Purpose

This document defines the current organizational structure of Kamiyon Studio, the responsibilities of each leadership role, and the principles that guide collaboration across the company.

As a growing startup, this structure is intentionally lean. Roles and responsibilities may evolve as the company expands, but the underlying philosophy of collaboration, ownership, and accountability should remain consistent.

---

# Organizational Philosophy

Kamiyon Studio is built around multidisciplinary collaboration rather than rigid departmental silos.

We believe that great products emerge when designers, programmers, artists, marketers, and community builders work closely together throughout the development process.

Leadership exists to enable collaboration, remove obstacles, and provide strategic direction—not to create unnecessary hierarchy.

---

# Organizational Principles

Our organizational structure is guided by the following principles:

* **Mission First** — Every role contributes to the company's mission.
* **Ownership** — Individuals take responsibility for their work and outcomes.
* **Collaboration** — Cross-functional teamwork is encouraged at every stage.
* **Transparency** — Decisions and communication should be open whenever appropriate.
* **Scalability** — The structure should support sustainable long-term growth.
* **Continuous Learning** — Every member is encouraged to grow beyond their current expertise.

# Cross-Functional Collaboration

Although responsibilities are clearly defined, no department operates in isolation.

Successful projects require continuous collaboration between:

* Leadership
* Engineering
* Design
* Art
* Marketing
* Community

Ideas are evaluated based on merit rather than job title.

---

# Decision-Making

Decision-making follows a balance between ownership and collaboration.

### Strategic Decisions

Led by the CEO with input from relevant leadership members.

Examples include:

* Company direction
* Major partnerships
* Long-term investments
* Organizational growth

---

### Technical Decisions

Led by the CTO in collaboration with the development team.

Examples include:

* Technology selection
* Software architecture
* Development tools
* Engineering standards

---

### Creative Decisions

Made collaboratively by the relevant product team, considering design goals, artistic direction, technical feasibility, and user experience.

---

### Marketing Decisions

Led by the CMO while ensuring alignment with Kamiyon's brand identity and mission.

# Leadership Expectations

Every leader at Kamiyon is expected to:

* Lead by example.
* Communicate openly.
* Support teammates.
* Encourage continuous learning.
* Protect the company's culture.
* Make decisions aligned with the mission.
* Accept responsibility for outcomes.

Leadership is measured by the success and growth of the team, not by authority alone.

# Organizational Evolution

Kamiyon recognizes that startups evolve rapidly.

The organizational structure described in this document is intended to support the company's current stage while remaining flexible enough to adapt as new opportunities emerge.

Future revisions may introduce new departments, leadership roles, or reporting structures, but should always preserve the company's collaborative culture and long-term vision.

---

# Final Principle

An organizational chart shows who is responsible for what.

It does not determine where ideas come from.

At Kamiyon Studio, innovation is everyone's responsibility.

Every team member—regardless of role—is encouraged to ask questions, propose ideas, solve problems, and contribute to creating meaningful experiences.

The strength of Kamiyon lies not in its hierarchy, but in the people who work together to bring its vision to life.
