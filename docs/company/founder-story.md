# Founder Story

> **Every company begins with an idea. Kamiyon Studio began with curiosity.**

---

# Introduction

Kamiyon Studio was never part of a carefully written business plan.

It didn't begin with investors, funding, or a grand strategy.

It began with two students who genuinely loved making games.

What started as a shared passion for game development eventually evolved into something much bigger: a studio dedicated to creating meaningful interactive experiences and proving that world-class products can come from the Philippines.

This document tells the story of how Kamiyon Studio came to be and the principles that continue to shape its future.

---

# The Beginning

In 2024, two Game Development students—**Sherwin Limosnero** and **Christian Jude Villaber**—shared a common passion: building games.

Like many aspiring developers, they started by participating in game jams, hackathons, and competitions. These events provided more than opportunities to win prizes; they became environments for rapid learning, experimentation, and collaboration.

Every competition introduced new constraints, new teammates, new technologies, and new lessons.

Rather than waiting until they felt "ready," they chose to learn by building.

---

# Learning Through Competition

Competitions became Kamiyon's first classroom.

Game jams taught the team how to:

* Build quickly under pressure.
* Prioritize ideas over perfection.
* Collaborate effectively.
* Iterate rapidly.
* Accept feedback.
* Finish projects.

Hackathons expanded that perspective beyond games.

They introduced real-world problems, emerging technologies, entrepreneurship, and multidisciplinary collaboration.

Instead of seeing games as isolated entertainment products, the founders began seeing them as powerful tools for education, communication, and problem-solving.

---

# A Growing Portfolio

Over time, every prototype, competition, and experimental project contributed to something larger.

Without realizing it at first, the founders had built a diverse portfolio spanning:

* Games
* Educational experiences
* Gamification
* Interactive applications
* Software prototypes
* Emerging technologies

These projects represented more than technical skills—they represented a mindset of continuous learning and experimentation.

---

# A Realization

As their portfolio grew, so did an important realization.

The knowledge they had accumulated through countless hours of building, failing, improving, and competing had value beyond competitions.

Organizations needed creative technology.

Students wanted mentors.

Communities needed accessible educational experiences.

Businesses wanted engaging digital solutions.

Instead of keeping their experience to themselves, the founders saw an opportunity to contribute to a much larger community.

---

# The Birth of Kamiyon Studio

Kamiyon Studio was created to transform years of experimentation into meaningful impact.

Rather than remaining a collection of side projects, the founders established a studio that could:

* Build games.
* Develop software.
* Design educational experiences.
* Help organizations innovate.
* Mentor aspiring creators.
* Invest in original intellectual property.

Client work would provide sustainability.

Original products would define the company's legacy.

---

# Why "Kamiyon"

The name **Kamiyon** comes from two words:

* **Kami** ("us")
* **'Yon** ("that")

Together, they express a simple but meaningful statement:

> **"That's us."**

Every project should reflect the team's creativity, values, craftsmanship, and identity.

Rather than chasing trends or imitating others, Kamiyon strives to create work that genuinely represents who its creators are.

---

# Building Beyond Games

Although game development remains at the heart of the company, the founders recognized that interactive technology extends far beyond entertainment.

Games can educate.

Software can inspire.

Gamification can transform learning.

Technology can strengthen communities.

This broader perspective shaped Kamiyon into a creative technology company rather than simply a game studio.

---

# A Different Definition of Success

From the beginning, Kamiyon chose not to define success solely by revenue.

Financial sustainability is essential, but it is not the company's ultimate purpose.

Success also means:

* Inspiring future Filipino creators.
* Helping organizations solve meaningful problems.
* Creating memorable experiences.
* Building original intellectual properties.
* Contributing positively to communities.
* Demonstrating that globally competitive products can be created in the Philippines.

This philosophy continues to influence every major decision.

# Lessons That Shaped the Studio

The founders' early experiences established several beliefs that continue to define Kamiyon.

## Build to Learn

Experience is gained through creating, testing, and improving—not waiting for perfect conditions.

---

## Finish What You Start

A completed prototype teaches more than an unfinished masterpiece.

Shipping builds experience.

---

## Collaboration Multiplies Creativity

The best ideas often emerge from diverse perspectives working toward a common goal.

Great products are rarely built alone.

---

## Technology Is a Means, Not an End

Whether using Unity, Godot, AI, blockchain, or future technologies, the focus should always remain on creating meaningful experiences.

Tools evolve.

Purpose endures.

---

## Every Project Matters

Even small prototypes contribute to long-term growth.

Every competition, experiment, and client project helps prepare the team for larger opportunities.

Nothing built with intention is ever wasted.

---

# Looking Toward the Future

Kamiyon Studio remains early in its journey.

The founders understand that becoming a globally respected creative company will take years of consistent work, learning, and adaptation.

The goal is not overnight success.

The goal is to build a company that continues creating meaningful experiences decades from now.

One game.

One partnership.

One lesson.

One community at a time.
