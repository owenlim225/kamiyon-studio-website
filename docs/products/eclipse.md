# Vision

Eclipse aims to deliver an exhilarating movement-focused platforming experience where mastery comes from skill, precision, and experimentation.

Rather than relying on combat as its primary source of engagement, Eclipse challenges players through movement, timing, and creative problem-solving.

The game should reward practice, curiosity, and continuous improvement.

---

# Core Concept

Eclipse is centered around a **dual-state world-swapping mechanic**.

Players navigate interconnected environments by switching between two states of the world in real time.

This mechanic forms the foundation of:

* Movement
* Puzzle solving
* Level design
* Exploration
* Player mastery

Every major gameplay system should reinforce this central idea.

---

# Core Gameplay Pillars

## Precision Movement

Movement should feel:

* Responsive
* Fast
* Fluid
* Predictable
* Skill-based

Player improvement should come from mastery rather than character progression alone.

---

## World Swapping

The dual-state mechanic is Eclipse's defining feature.

The two world states should create opportunities for:

* Alternate routes
* Platform transitions
* Environmental puzzles
* Speedrunning strategies
* Exploration

The mechanic should feel intuitive to learn while offering long-term depth.

---

## Flow

The game should encourage uninterrupted movement.

Well-designed levels should allow experienced players to chain actions together into satisfying sequences.

Flow is one of Eclipse's defining emotional experiences.

---

## Discovery

Players should be rewarded for experimenting.

Hidden routes, optional challenges, shortcuts, and advanced movement techniques should encourage replayability and exploration.

---

# Target Audience

Eclipse is designed primarily for:

* Players who enjoy movement-focused platformers.
* Speedrunners.
* Completionists.
* Players who enjoy mechanical mastery.
* Indie game enthusiasts.

While approachable for newcomers, the game should provide enough depth to reward dedicated players.

---

# Design Philosophy

Eclipse follows several guiding principles.

* Easy to learn, difficult to master.
* Mechanics before spectacle.
* Movement should always feel rewarding.
* Challenge should feel fair.
* Failure should encourage experimentation.
* Every level should teach something new.

The game should respect the player's time while continuously encouraging improvement.

---

# Artistic Direction

The visual style should prioritize:

* Strong silhouettes.
* Readable environments.
* Clear gameplay communication.
* Memorable visual identity.
* Smooth animation.

Art should support gameplay clarity before visual complexity.

---

# Audio Direction

Audio should reinforce movement and player feedback.

Examples include:

* Responsive movement sounds.
* Distinct world-swap cues.
* Environmental ambience.
* Memorable musical themes.

Every sound should improve the player's understanding of the game.

# Technical Goals

Eclipse should strive for:

* Stable performance.
* Responsive controls.
* Low input latency.
* Fast loading.
* Modular architecture.
* Cross-platform flexibility where practical.

Technical decisions should always prioritize player experience.
