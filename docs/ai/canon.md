# Kamiyon Canon

> **This document defines the hierarchy of truth for Kamiyon Studio. It establishes which information is considered canonical, how conflicts should be resolved, and how AI systems and team members should interpret and extend the studio's documentation.**

# Purpose

The Kamiyon Canon is the authoritative knowledge system for the studio.

Its purpose is to ensure that every document, presentation, proposal, website, marketing asset, AI-generated response, and internal discussion is based on a single, consistent source of truth.

The canon exists to preserve continuity as Kamiyon Studio grows.

---

# What Is Canon?

A piece of information is considered **canonical** when it has been intentionally documented and approved within the Kamiyon Canon repository.

Canonical information should be treated as factual unless it is explicitly updated or replaced.

Examples include:

* Company mission
* Vision
* Core values
* Brand identity
* Product information
* Official services
* Team roles
* Design philosophy
* Technology philosophy

---

# Hierarchy of Truth

When multiple sources provide conflicting information, use the following order of precedence.

## Level 1 — User Instructions

Direct instructions from Kamiyon Studio leadership for the current task take the highest priority.

These instructions may temporarily override the canon for the specific task.

Unless explicitly stated, they should **not** automatically modify the canon.

---

## Level 2 — Canonical Documents

The documents within the `kamiyon-canon` repository are the official long-term source of truth.

If information exists here, it should be preferred over memory, assumptions, or previous drafts.

---

## Level 3 — Supporting Materials

Examples include:

* Presentation decks
* Website copy
* Social media content
* Marketing campaigns
* Proposal documents
* Internal notes

These should align with the canon but are not authoritative if conflicts arise.

---

## Level 4 — Suggestions

Ideas, brainstorming sessions, drafts, and recommendations are **not** canonical until they are intentionally incorporated into the repository.

AI should clearly distinguish suggestions from established facts.

---

# Canonical Principles

Every canonical document should support at least one of the following:

* Consistency
* Clarity
* Accuracy
* Maintainability
* Long-term thinking
* Educational value

Documentation should remain useful years after it is written.

---

# What AI May Assume

AI may reasonably infer:

* Formatting consistency.
* Cross-references between documents.
* Existing terminology.
* Established company philosophy.

AI **should not** invent:

* New products.
* New services.
* Partnerships.
* Awards.
* Statistics.
* Employees.
* Clients.
* Historical events.
* Financial information.

When information is missing, AI should ask for clarification.

---

# Updating the Canon

The canon is designed to evolve.

Updates should occur when:

* The company changes direction.
* New products are announced.
* Services expand.
* Team structure changes.
* Brand identity evolves.
* Strategic decisions are finalized.

Updates should replace outdated information rather than create conflicting versions.

---

# Versioning Philosophy

The canon should represent the **current** state of Kamiyon Studio.

Historical documents may be archived separately if they remain valuable, but active documents should always reflect the latest approved information.

Avoid maintaining multiple conflicting versions of the same concept.

---

# Writing Standards

Canonical documents should be:

* Clear.
* Structured.
* Professional.
* Concise where possible.
* Timeless.
* Internally consistent.

Whenever possible:

* Define concepts before explaining them.
* Avoid unnecessary repetition.
* Cross-reference related documents.
* Focus on enduring principles instead of temporary trends.

---

# Cross-Referencing

Documents should complement—not duplicate—one another.

For example:

* Mission belongs in `mission-vision.md`.
* Brand voice belongs in `tone-of-voice.md`.
* Engineering practices belong in `engineering-principles.md`.

Other documents should reference these sources rather than restate them in full.

This reduces maintenance and improves consistency.

---

# Canon Maintenance

Every significant addition should answer the following questions:

1. Is this factual?
2. Is it approved?
3. Does it already exist elsewhere?
4. Is it timeless?
5. Will this still be useful several years from now?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, the information may belong in a project document rather than the canon.

---

# Responsibilities

The Kamiyon leadership team is responsible for maintaining the canon.

AI assists by:

* Organizing information.
* Identifying inconsistencies.
* Suggesting improvements.
* Maintaining formatting.
* Preserving terminology.

AI should never silently modify canonical facts.

---

# Scope of the Canon

The Kamiyon Canon is intended to support:

* Company strategy.
* Brand identity.
* Product development.
* Engineering.
* Marketing.
* Client services.
* Partnerships.
* Community initiatives.
* AI-assisted workflows.

It is not intended to document temporary project management tasks, sprint planning, or short-lived operational details.

---

# Guiding Philosophy

The canon should capture **why** Kamiyon operates the way it does—not only **what** it currently does.

Technologies, products, and processes will evolve.

The studio's mission, values, philosophy, and identity should remain the foundation that guides those changes.

---

# Success Criteria

The canon is successful when:

* Team members can onboard quickly.
* AI produces consistent outputs.
* Documentation remains easy to maintain.
* Brand messaging stays aligned.
* Strategic decisions reference established principles.
* New knowledge can be integrated without creating contradictions.

A successful canon reduces ambiguity while encouraging thoughtful evolution.

---

# Final Principle

The Kamiyon Canon is more than a collection of documents.

It is the institutional memory of the studio.

As Kamiyon grows, people, technologies, products, and markets will change. The canon preserves the ideas, principles, and identity that make Kamiyon Studio recognizable across every project, every platform, and every generation of its team.

When in doubt, preserve clarity, preserve consistency, and preserve the mission.
