# Purpose

This document establishes the strategic guidelines for designing, writing, and maintaining the official Kamiyon Studio website.

The website should consistently communicate the company's identity while serving multiple audiences, including prospective clients, partners, future employees, media, and players.

The primary goal is to build trust and encourage meaningful action.

---

# Website Objectives

The website should:

* Clearly explain who Kamiyon Studio is.
* Showcase original products.
* Present professional services.
* Build credibility.
* Generate qualified leads.
* Highlight the team's expertise.
* Demonstrate thought leadership.
* Support community initiatives.
* Reflect the Kamiyon brand.

Every page should contribute toward at least one of these objectives.

---

# Primary Audience

The website is primarily designed for:

* Prospective clients.
* Business partners.
* Educational institutions.
* Web3 organizations.
* Investors.
* Community organizations.
* Future team members.
* Media.
* Players interested in our original games.

Content should remain accessible to both technical and non-technical visitors.

---

# Brand Experience

Visitors should leave the website feeling that Kamiyon Studio is:

* Professional.
* Creative.
* Approachable.
* Trustworthy.
* Innovative.
* Educational.
* Forward-thinking.
* Passionate about quality.

The experience should reflect our brand personality from the first interaction.

---

# Website Structure

The website should include, at minimum, the following sections.

## Home

Introduce Kamiyon Studio through:

* Clear value proposition.
* Mission.
* Featured work.
* Call-to-action.
* Company highlights.

The homepage should answer:

**Who are we?**

**What do we do?**

**Why should someone work with us?**

---

## About

Explain:

* Company story.
* Mission and vision.
* Core values.
* Team.
* Company culture.

The About page should help visitors connect with the people behind the studio.

---

## Services

Present our professional offerings, including:

* Game Development
* MVP Development
* Gamification
* Web Development
* Mobile Development
* UI/UX Design
* AI Integration
* Blockchain Solutions
* Consultation
* Creative Services

Each service should focus on outcomes rather than technical jargon.

---

## Products

Showcase Kamiyon's original intellectual property.

Each product should include:

* Overview.
* Screenshots.
* Trailer (when available).
* Development status.
* Key features.
* Project goals.

Original products reinforce Kamiyon's creative identity.

---

## Portfolio

Display completed client projects, prototypes, game jam entries, and other notable work.

Each case study should emphasize:

* The challenge.
* The solution.
* The impact.
* Lessons learned.

Focus on storytelling rather than simply listing projects.

---

## Community

Highlight:

* Workshops.
* Hackathons.
* Game jams.
* Speaking engagements.
* Educational initiatives.
* Partnerships.
* Community involvement.

Community engagement is a core part of Kamiyon's identity.

---

## Contact

Provide clear ways for visitors to:

* Book a consultation.
* Request a proposal.
* Ask questions.
* Explore partnerships.

The contact experience should be simple and welcoming.

---

# Calls-to-Action

Every major page should include a clear next step.

Examples include:

* Book a Discovery Call.
* Start Your Project.
* Explore Our Services.
* View Our Portfolio.
* Learn More About Our Games.
* Partner With Us.
* Contact Our Team.

Calls-to-action should feel inviting rather than aggressive.

---

# Content Guidelines

Website copy should be:

* Clear.
* Concise.
* Helpful.
* Professional.
* Educational.
* Human.

Avoid unnecessary buzzwords and overly technical language unless writing for a technical audience.

---

# Visual Guidelines

The website should consistently reflect Kamiyon's visual identity.

This includes:

* Sakura-inspired branding.
* Clean layouts.
* Premium presentation.
* Playful but professional aesthetics.
* Strong typography hierarchy.
* Accessible color contrast.
* High-quality imagery.
* Consistent spacing.

Visual design should support readability before decoration.

---

# User Experience Principles

Navigation should be:

* Simple.
* Predictable.
* Accessible.
* Mobile-friendly.
* Fast.
* Intuitive.

Visitors should always know where they are and what to do next.

Every interaction should reduce friction.

---

# Trust Signals

The website should communicate credibility through:

* Team introductions.
* Case studies.
* Published products.
* Client testimonials (when available).
* Community involvement.
* Awards and competition achievements.
* Professional partnerships.

Trust should be earned through evidence rather than exaggerated claims.

---

# Search Engine Considerations

Content should be structured to support discoverability.

Pages should:

* Have descriptive titles.
* Use meaningful headings.
* Clearly describe services.
* Include relevant keywords naturally.
* Prioritize readability over keyword density.

SEO should enhance user experience, not compromise it.

---

# Performance Standards

The website should strive for:

* Fast loading times.
* Responsive layouts.
* Accessible navigation.
* Optimized media.
* Reliable uptime.
* Modern web standards.

Performance is part of the user experience.

---

# Long-Term Evolution

As Kamiyon grows, the website should evolve to include:

* New original IP.
* Expanded service offerings.
* Educational resources.
* Blog articles.
* Careers.
* Press kit.
* Developer insights.
* Community programs.

The website should remain a living representation of the studio.

---

# Relationship to Our Mission

The website is often the first interaction someone has with Kamiyon Studio.

It should immediately communicate our commitment to creating games and interactive experiences that educate, inspire, and make a lasting impact.

Every page should reinforce our values, demonstrate our capabilities, and invite visitors to become part of our journey.

---

# Final Principle

The Kamiyon Studio website should not feel like a digital brochure.

It should feel like stepping into the studio itself—welcoming, thoughtfully designed, creatively inspiring, and built with the same attention to detail that we bring to every product and partnership.

If visitors leave the website with a clear understanding of who we are, what we believe, and why our work matters, then it has fulfilled its purpose.
